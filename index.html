About Me

I watch atoms move.

I am an electron microscopy specialist and materials scientist at the University of Bristol. Using operando TEM, I observe how catalysts transform disordered carbon into graphene in real time, and I use that understanding to turn waste plastic into valuable materials.

Over 12 years across Nepal, Japan, and the UK, I have studied ten different metal catalyst systems and pioneered waste-to-graphene CVD (Carbon 2014, 210+ citations). At Cambridge, I led EU Graphene Flagship work packages, and I currently build AI tools for automated diffraction analysis. My research has resulted in 40+ publications, >1,000 citations, and an h-index of 19.

Core Research Interests

🔬 Advanced Electron Microscopy & Operando TEM

My work pushes the boundaries of nanoscale observation by visualizing reactions as they happen. I specialize in designing and conducting in-situ heating and biasing experiments.

  • Achieved atomic-level resolution of individual Li atoms without cryogenic conditions.
  • Extensive expertise with JEOL JEM ARM 200F and FEI Osiris systems.
⚛️ 2D Materials Synthesis

I have extensive experience in the scalable growth of next-generation materials, driven by my work at the Cambridge Graphene Centre and Nagoya Institute of Technology.

  • Wafer-scale (200mm) CVD synthesis of graphene, hBN, and TMDCs.
  • Pioneered waste-to-graphene CVD techniques (Carbon 2014, 210+ citations).
🤖 AI-Based Materials Discovery

Bridging materials science with artificial intelligence to accelerate the analysis of massive characterization datasets.

  • Building AI tools for automated diffraction analysis.
  • Applying machine learning models to optimize catalyst-mediated carbon transformations.
