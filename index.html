About Me

I am a materials scientist with expertise in large-area graphene and 2D materials synthesis. With 12+ years of experience in Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM), I have extensively studied metal-carbon interactions during nanoscale graphene formation. At the Cambridge Graphene Centre, I worked on the large-scale growth of 2D materials, including graphene, hexagonal boron nitride (hBN), and transition metal dichalcogenides.

My core interests include battery and energy storage technologies, where I successfully developed a technique to achieve atomic-level resolution of individual Li atoms without the use of cryogenic conditions in TEM. I am an innovative, logical thinker with a proven track record of leadership, ready to apply scientific reasoning to complex academic and industrial challenges.
